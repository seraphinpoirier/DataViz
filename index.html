Data Visualizations

Visualizing Conflict and Human Suffering through Data

Group: Data Vizionaries

Project by
Stefano Infusini (S5211120), Aymen M. Saney (S6021940) & Séraphin Poirier (S9259977)

Introduction

The world is a violent place plagued by conflict. This project aims to help users realise the different types of violences which have been inflicted to civilians in these past years. Through interactive data visualizations, we explore the tragic reality of modern conflicts, their human cost, and patterns that emerge from systematic data collection.

About the Data

This visualization project utilizes mainly uses the following data source to provide a comprehensive view of conflict and human suffering:

  • ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project): Real-time data collection of political violence and protest events worldwide.

The datasets used in this project cover various aspects of conflict including:

  1. Fatalities by conflict type and region
  2. Civilian vs. combatant casualties
  3. Geographic distribution of conflicts

Project Sections

Section 1 —Comparisons

This section presents five mandatory visualizations exploring conflict data from multiple perspectives, plus one extra:

  • Bar Chart: Fatalities by conflict type across different regions
  • Grouped Bar Chart: Comparison of civilian and combatant casualties over time
  • Heatmap: Temporal patterns of conflict events by region
  • 100% Stacked Bar Chart: Proportion of different violence types within each region
  • Waffle Chart: Distribution of Event Types
  • [EXTRA] Choropleth Map: Geographic Distribution of Conflicts

Each visualization is interactive, allowing users to explore the data in detail. Hover over elements to see specific values and uncover patterns.

Section 2 — Distributions

This section focuses on how fatalities and events are distributed across time and geography, highlighting inequality and concentration of conflict impact.

  • Histogram: Distribution of event-level fatality counts
  • Ridgeline Plot: Density of fatalities across regions
  • Boxplot: Distribution of fatalities over the years, including medians and outliers

These visualizations allow users to examine the spread, inequality, and variability within conflict data.
