Exploring Conflict Data Through Interactive Charts

Interactive Data Exploration

This page presents a comprehensive set of visualizations examining conflict and human suffering data. Each chart provides unique insights into different aspects of violence, casualties, and their patterns across time and geography. Use the interactive features to explore the data in detail.

Top Countries by Total Fatalities

This bar chart displays the top 10 countries with the highest total reported fatalities from conflicts, aggregated across all years in the dataset. It helps identify which countries have experienced the most severe human cost from political violence.

Civilian vs Combatant Fatalities by Year

The grouped bar chart compares reported civilian and combatant fatalities over recent years (2018-2024), highlighting the disproportionate impact of conflicts on civilian populations. Combatant fatalities are calculated as the difference between total and civilian fatalities.

Events Targeting Civilians by Region and Year

This heatmap visualizes the intensity of events targeting civilians across different regions throughout recent years, revealing temporal patterns and regional hotspots of violence against civilians. Data is aggregated by region (Middle East & Asia, Africa, Europe, Americas) for the years 2018-2024.

Distribution of Event Types by Region

The 100% stacked bar chart shows the proportional breakdown of different event types within each region, including events targeting civilians, demonstration events, and other conflict-related events. This allows for relative comparisons across geographical areas to understand the nature of violence in each region.

Distribution of Event Types

Most recorded political events in the world are demonstrations, not direct attacks on civilians.

Geographic Distribution of Conflicts

Countries are shaded by the total number of conflict events affecting civilians (events targeting civilians + demonstration events, summed across years).
