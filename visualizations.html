Data Visualizations

Exploring Conflict Data Through Interactive Charts

Interactive Data Exploration

This page presents a comprehensive set of visualizations examining conflict and human suffering data. Use the interactive features to explore the data in detail.

Section 1 — Comparisons

Comparing categories across countries, years, and regions

In this section we use classic comparison-oriented charts (bars, grouped bars, heatmaps) to contrast categories and time periods, highlighting where and when civilians and combatants are impacted most.

Top Countries by Total Fatalities

Top 10 countries with the highest total reported fatalities across all years.

Civilian vs Combatant Fatalities by Year

Civilian and combatant fatalities (2018–2024). Combatants are approximated as total minus civilian fatalities.

Events Targeting Civilians by Region and Year

Intensity of events targeting civilians by region (Middle East & Asia, Africa, Europe, Americas) and year (2018–2024).

Distribution of Event Types by Region

100% stacked bars show each region’s mix of event types (targets on civilians, demonstrations, other).

Distribution of Event Types

Most recorded political events are demonstrations rather than direct attacks on civilians.

Geographic Distribution of Conflicts

Countries shaded by the total number of conflict events affecting civilians (events targeting civilians + demonstrations).

Section 2 — Distributions

How values spread: histograms, violin/ridgeline, and boxplots

This section focuses on the shape and spread of the data. You’ll see one histogram (a population pyramid also works), one violin plot (ridgeline also works), and one boxplot.

Histogram of Event Magnitudes

Distribution of event-level fatality counts. For a demographic split, this can be rendered as a population pyramid.

Violin Plot of Fatalities by Region

Density and spread of event fatalities across regions. A ridgeline plot can be used as an alternative to show overlapping distributions by year.

Boxplot of Fatalities by Year

Median, quartiles, and outliers for event fatalities across years.
